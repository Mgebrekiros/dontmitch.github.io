About Me

I was fortunate enough to know from a young age that I wanted to be an engineer; the engineering way of thinking came naturally to me. What I didn't know was what type of engineer I wanted to be. When push came to shove, I chose mechanical engineering because it was the most noncommittal.

Truth be told, what I really enjoy, and what I really want to do, is learn. When I graduated from Northwestern and took a job as a stress analyst at Boeing Defense, I loved it. I was learning every single day. I was watching military helicopters - my daily work - fly overhead on my walk in. How could anyone not love that?

But then, in late 2012, the realization that I was becoming too specialized too quickly washed over me. Less than two years into my job and I already had two patents to my name. I was useful to no more than five or six other companies in the world. And that meant that, as a 23 year old, I was immobile.

After some introspection, I decided to pivot away from that career to something else that I loved equally as much: programming. A year later, I realized that relegating my learning to nights and weekends wasn't going to get me a job as a programmer. I need something more.

So I turned to Dev Bootcamp, and learned more than I could ever have imagined was possible in nine (actually twelve - there was an optional fourth phase that I couldn't resist) weeks.

And now - now I am a junior developer eager (and when I say eager, I mean EAGER ) to continue learning as fast as my mind will allow me to learn.

My Interests

If my about About Me did not get this point accross, allow me to bash you over the head with it: I love learning. To that end, many of my interests involve learning or, relatedly, experiencing new things. Traveling is a great example. In 2012 I went on over two dozen trips, because I love traveling and seeing new places. I love eating at places that I've never eaten at - in fact, I tend to avoid places that I've already been once.

I'd rather read a book or skim the coverage of a new technology than watch TV. I'd rather build something using a raspberry pi than catch up on celebrity gossip. I'd rather solve logic problems than think about fashion. No one option is better than the other, but that's just who I am.

Oh, and I also love triathlons.
